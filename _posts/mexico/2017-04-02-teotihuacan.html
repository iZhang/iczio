Mexico City: Lucky Mole

  • #TOPPWB
  • 22 January 2017


I learned a lot of lessons in Mexico City after an impromptu weekend trip. I'll dish just the highlights here:

  1. There's levels to this domicile life. Upon check-in to our hotel, we were upgraded to a 2-floor apartment. This place was so large we had a servant's entrance to the full kitchen. Every morning we had, in the words of my friend Max, a literal orchard delivered fresh to our room. I need to raise my level.
  2. There's levels to this food life. Pujol, a molecular gastronomy spot led by Chef's Table featured Enrique Olvera, called to inform us they had a 6-seater cancel that night at 8 pm, and if we could get there it was ours. We called that morning at 11 am. For reference, if you tried to book today, you wouldn't get a table until March. We hightailed it from the D Tap Room in Roma back to Polanco for baby corn dusted with mayo and ants, three year aged mole, and ascendance to heaven. I need to raise my what-i-put-in-my-mouf life.
  3. There's level to this movement life. We drunkenly stumbled into Mama Rumba with no preparation. I need to raise my rhythm life.
